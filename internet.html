History of the Internet


1969

The first version of the Internet was called ARPANET or Advanced Research Projects Agency Network was created by the United States Department of Defense. The first internet was comprised of four computers. One at UCLA, One at Stanford research institute, One at UC Santa Barbar and one at university of Utah. The first message ever sent was "Lo". This was supposed to read login but stanfords computer crashed after two letters.


Email is first introduced by BBN's Ray Tomlinson and creates a network for it. He also created standard protocols for adresses. Then The college of London links it's computer to ARPANET and so does the Royal Radar Establishment. This is what really makes the internet or a global network. The first ISP pops up in the form of Telenet which was released as a commerical version of ARPANET. In 1976 Queen elizebeth II sends her first Email. USENET is created to host news and discussion groups.

1980s

The national Science Foundation (NSF) creates Computer Science Network (CSNET) to provide networking to universities. TCP/IP was created as a standard protocol for ARPANET and is still used today. In 1983 domain names are created to help users find websites easier. 1986 was when NSFNET was created which was essentially all runnig off of a single 56,000 bit computer. This became a network of networks connecting academic institues throughout the country.


In 1990 Tim Berners-Lee, a scientist at CERN, created Hyper Text Markup Language or HTML. This largly changes the way websites are devoloped and is still used largely today. The next year CERN unviels that World Wide Web to the public. By 1993 NSFNET has a total of 2 million users. The first Operating Systems start being released Such as windows 95. Lastly google is born in 1998 changing the way users interact with the internet.
